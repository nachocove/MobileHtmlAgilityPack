Interested in building community around a Web technology? W3C has created Community Groups and Business Groups so that developers, designers, and anyone passionate about the Web has a place to have discussions and publish documents.

A W3C Community Group is an open forum, without fees, where Web developers and other stakeholders develop specifications, hold discussions, develop test suites, and connect with W3C's international community of Web experts.

A W3C Business Group gives innovators that want to have an impact on the development of the Web in the near-term a vendor-neutral forum for collaborating with like-minded stakeholders, including W3C Members and non-Members.

Learn more about Community and Business Groups, the lightweight process that governs them, and consult the Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ).

How do I start a group?

Anyone with a W3C account may create a group:

  1. Push one of the buttons below and provide a name and description for the group.
  2. Once four other people support the group (by pushing a button), W3C creates it.
  3. Join the group and start using the tools and infrastructure to build your community.
