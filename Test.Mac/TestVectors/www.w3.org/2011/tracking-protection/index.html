Tracking Protection Working Group

The Tracking Protection Working Group is chartered to improve user privacy and user control by defining mechanisms for expressing user preferences around Web tracking and for blocking or allowing Web tracking elements. The group seeks to standardize the technology and meaning of Do Not Track, and of Tracking Selection Lists.

Carl Cargill (Adobe), Justin Brookman (CDT) and Matthias Schunter (Intel) serve as co-chairs of the Tracking Protection Working Group. The W3C Team Contacts are Nick Doty and Ninja Marnau.

Call for Comments has ended

Input Documents

The chairs invite input documents for the Working Group to consider at its initial meeting. Please send possible documents to the working group mailing list. The following (non-exhaustive) list of relevant documents has been identified.

  • The FTC staff report that calls on industry to create a Do Not Track mechanism
  • FTC guidelines for behavioral targeting
  • IAB Self-Regulatory Principles for Online Behavioral Advertising
  • Neelie Kroes, EU Commission, Online privacy - reinforcing trust and confidence
  • Do Not Track Cookbook: approaches to data collection in DNT-friendly ways
  • IETF proposal for Do Not Track
  • Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) discussion of Do Not Track
  • The Center for Democracy and Technology (CDT) DNT proposal
  • Mozilla Do Not Track Field Guide
  • Microsoft Web Tracking Protection, member submission to W3C

Additionally, please refer to the report and position papers from the W3C Workshop on Web Tracking and User Privacy in April 2011.

We invite the submission of use cases and input documents to the group mailing list.

For further information, please contact the Team Contact for the Tracking Protection Working Group:
Nick Doty (npdoty@w3.org)
